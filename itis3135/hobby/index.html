What

I’m a car enthusiast who enjoys German performance cars and modern V8s. I follow builds, learn about maintenance, and like simple, tasteful mods that make a car drive better.

  • Performance sedans and coupes
  • Light mods (intake, tune, suspension)
  • Enjoying weekend drives and car meets

Who

I’m Vahid. I like learning from owners, DIY resources, and local meetups. The car community is helpful if you’re respectful and curious.

  • Friends who wrench and drive
  • Local meet groups
  • Online forums and how-to guides

How I participate

Mostly weekend drives, light mods, and sharing tips with friends.

When

Best times are weekend mornings or cool evenings. Bigger meets usually pop up in spring and fall.

  • Sat/Sun mornings: drives & coffee
  • Weeknights: quick DIY tasks
  • Spring/Fall: most events

Simple routine

Plan a route, check fluids/tires, drive safely, and be respectful to neighborhoods.

Where

I look for smooth roads, safe pull-offs, and spots with good lighting for photos. Local tracks and cars-and-coffee meetups are great too.

  • Scenic parkways
  • Cars & coffee lots
  • Track days (beginner groups)

Finding locations

Ask friends, check local groups, and save map pins so you can return later.

How

Getting started

  1. Set a budget and goal (daily, track, show).
  2. Start with basic maintenance.
  3. Add simple, reversible mods.

Example easy mods

  • Performance tires
  • Brake pads/fluids
  • Mild tune (research first)

Why

I enjoy the engineering, the driving feel, and the community. Cars are a hands-on way to learn and make friends.

  • Mechanical learning
  • Personal expression
  • Memorable drives

Keep it simple

Do small steps, stay safe, and enjoy the process.
