Client & Project Category

Client Category (Step 01): Student group at UNC Charlotte
Project Category (Step 02): Student group site that encourages participation and communication

The 49ers Car Culture Club (C3) is a student organization for UNC Charlotte students who are interested in cars, driving, and community events. This website will help them share information, organize events, and attract new members.

1) Application Purpose

The C3 website will:

  • Promote upcoming meets, cruises, and tech nights.
  • Show what the club is about, including photos and event highlights.
  • Help new students learn how to join and what to expect.
  • Provide a simple way to contact the officers for questions or partnerships.

2) Intended Users

  • Prospective members: Students curious about joining the club.
  • Active members: Students already in the club who need event details.
  • Officers: Students who organize events and share information.
  • Partners / Sponsors: Local shops or organizations interested in working with the club.

3) Content Overview

  • Home: Short intro, next event highlight, and quick links.
  • About: Mission, officer bios, and safety / behavior guidelines.
  • Events: List of upcoming and recent events (read-only list).
  • Gallery: Photo gallery with car meet pictures (interactive images).
  • Join & Contact: Interest form and basic contact information.
  • Optional: Resources or FAQ page with helpful links and rules.

7) Required Pages & Navigation

  • Home (index.html): Intro, next event, quick CTA to join.
  • About (about.html): Mission, officer information, and rules.
  • Events (events.html): List of upcoming and past events.
  • Gallery (gallery.html): Interactive photo gallery of meets.
  • Join & Contact (join.html): Interest form and contact info.
  • Optional: Resources (resources.html): Links, policies, FAQ.

All pages will share a common header and footer (via includes) and a consistent navigation bar that links to each of the main pages in a single click.

8) Page Design Details

Home (index.html)
  • Purpose: Welcome users and highlight the next event.
  • Audience: All users (new and returning).
  • Content: Hero text, brief description, next event card, gallery preview, join button.
  • Inputs: None.
  • Actions: Buttons/links to Events and Join & Contact pages.
About (about.html)
  • Purpose: Explain what C3 is and who runs it.
  • Audience: Prospective members and partners.
  • Content: Mission statement, officer bios, and safety / behavior guidelines.
  • Inputs: None.
  • Actions: Links to Join & Contact and Events.
Events (events.html)
  • Purpose: Show upcoming and recent events.
  • Audience: Mostly active members.
  • Content: Event cards with date, time, location, and short description.
  • Inputs: None (read-only list).
  • Actions: Filter or highlight events using simple JavaScript (e.g., show only upcoming).
Gallery (gallery.html)
  • Purpose: Show photos from meets and cruises.
  • Audience: Everyone, especially prospective members.
  • Content: Image grid with hover or click interaction.
  • Inputs: None (no uploads; static images only).
  • Actions: Clicking an image opens a larger view (lightbox style) with JavaScript.
Join & Contact (join.html)
  • Purpose: Let students express interest and contact officers.
  • Audience: Prospective members and partners.
  • Content: Form fields for name, email, car interest, and message; basic contact info; maybe a Google Map to campus.
  • Inputs: Form fields (validated on the client with JavaScript).
  • Actions: On submit, show a confirmation message (no database; read-only behavior).

9) Dynamic Functionality & Interactivity

The website will use JavaScript and jQuery (and optionally jQuery-UI) for light interactivity. Planned features include:

  • Responsive navigation: Simple mobile menu toggle.
  • Events filtering: Show only upcoming events or toggle between upcoming / past events.
  • Gallery lightbox: Click an image to view a larger version with a caption.
  • Form validation: Check that required fields on the Join & Contact page are filled in correctly.
  • jQuery-UI widget: Possible use of an accordion or tabs for FAQs or event details.

Example sites that inspired this interactivity:

  • University club sites with event calendars and galleries.
  • Simple car meet pages that show photos and basic event information.
